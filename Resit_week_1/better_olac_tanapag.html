Introduction


The Tanapag language is an indigenous language spoken in the Northern Mariana Islands, specifically on the island of Saipan. It belongs to the Austronesian language family and is part of the Chamorro language group. Tanapag is primarily spoken by the Chamorro people, although the number of native speakers has declined over time due to the dominance of English and Chamorro. Efforts are being made to preserve and revitalize the language through educational programs and cultural initiatives.
