Quechua Language Entry

Language Name: Quechua

ISO 639-3 Code: que

Language Family: Quechuan

Region: South America (Peru, Bolivia, Ecuador, Colombia, Argentina, Chile)

Number of Speakers: Approximately 8-10 million

About the Language

Quechua is an indigenous language spoken by millions of people in the Andean region of South America. It was the primary language of the Inca Empire and remains an important part of Andean culture today.

Writing System

Quechua is written using the Latin script, and its orthography varies slightly between different countries.

Example Phrase

English: Hello, how are you?

Quechua: Rimaykullayki, imanallataq kashanki?
